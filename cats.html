Cats vs. Rocks vs. Gerbils

As pets — an honest comparison

🐈 Cats

Type
Real companion animal
Lifespan
12–18 years
Cost
~$50–100/month baseline, plus occasional large vet bills
Interaction
Affectionate on their own terms; form real attachments
Travel fit
Tolerate absences with a sitter, but notice them
Commitment
Long — you're signing up for a lasting relationship

🪨 Rocks

Type
Pet rock
Lifespan
Infinite
Cost
$0 — no food, no vet, no litter
Interaction
None. Zero emotional reciprocation
Travel fit
Perfect — fully travel-compatible
Commitment
Zero maintenance; fails as "pet" in any real sense

🐹 Gerbils

Type
Actual small pet
Lifespan
3–4 years
Cost
Cheap — ~$20/month
Interaction
Minimal; more "watch them" than bond with them. Social — should be kept in same-sex pairs, not alone
Travel fit
Easier to board or leave with a neighbor
Commitment
Low day-to-day maintenance

Bottom line for your situation

With 3–6 months of travel per year and a "max life / min stress" constraint:

Gerbils are a bad fit because of the travel. Cats are workable but require a reliable sitter network and add a recurring logistical variable to every trip. Rocks are genuinely the lowest-friction option — which matters if simplicity is the real constraint.

If you want a real pet that tolerates your lifestyle, the honest answer isn't on this list — it's something like a bonded cat pair with a trusted sitter, or no pet and borrowing other people's.
